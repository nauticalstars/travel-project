Why Whistler?

One of the largest ski resorts in North America

Whistler is a town north of Vancouver, British Columbia, that's home to Whistler Blackcomb, one of the largest ski resorts in North America. Besides skiing and snowboarding, the area offers snowshoeing, tobogganing and ski jumping at the Olympic Park, a venue for the 2010 Vancouver Winter Olympics. The hub of Whistler is a compact, chalet-style pedestrian village at the base of Whistler and Blackcomb mountains.

Cows Ice Cream

COWS began to make ice cream in 1983 with an old-fashioned family recipe that originated in Cavendish, Prince Edward Island. In a very short time, the demand for COWS Ice Cream and products increased dramatically, and COWS quickly became a Prince Edward Island symbol. Despite the growth that COWS experienced, the same old-fashioned ingredients are still used today when making the 32+ flavours that are produced in Charlottetown. The ice cream is handmade and made of using the highest quality ingredients, including 16% butterfat. Our old-fashioned recipe is churned slowly, adding limited amounts of air so each bite of ice cream is creamy and packed with flavour! Every scoop contains fresh cream, real eggs, and is served in a handmade waffle cone.
